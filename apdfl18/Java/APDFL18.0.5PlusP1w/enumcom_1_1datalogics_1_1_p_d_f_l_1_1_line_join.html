Detailed Description

The line join style specifies the shape to be used at the corners of paths that are stroked. Join styles are significant only at points where consecutive segments of a path connect at an angle; segments that meet or intersect fortuitously receive no special treatment.

Member Data Documentation

◆ BEVEL_JOIN

com.datalogics.PDFL.LineJoin.BEVEL_JOIN

The two segments are finished with butt caps and the resulting notch beyond the ends of the segments is filled with a triangle.

◆ MITER_JOIN

com.datalogics.PDFL.LineJoin.MITER_JOIN

The outer edges of the strokes for the two segments are extended until they meet at an angle, as in a picture frame. If the segments meet at too sharp an angle, a bevel join is used instead.

◆ ROUND_JOIN

com.datalogics.PDFL.LineJoin.ROUND_JOIN

An arc of a circle with a diameter equal to the line width is drawn around the point where the two segments meet, connecting the outer edges of the strokes for the two segments. This pieslice-shaped figure is filled in, producing a rounded corner.
